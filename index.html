Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q. How can I create an IRCTC account login ID?

A: To create a new IRCTC login ID, visit the official home page of IRCTC. Right below the login box, there is an option to sign up. When you click on it, a registration box will open. Now enter your user ID, password, security question, and preferred language, followed by personal information like name, gender, Aadhaar card number, PAN card number and your residential address. After you enter all the information, click on submit. For the verification of your IRCTC login account, a link will be sent to your registered email ID. Once the registration process is complete you can use your IRCTC account.

Q. Can I change my IRCTC login ID?

A: For IRCTC login, open the ixigo app and just log in using your registered username. Next, select the departure and arrival destinations, date of travel, the class you want to travel in, and your traveller details. Then, click on 'Pay Now' to complete the payment. Once it is successful, the app will ask you to enter your password to complete your booking.

Q. What is the maximum number of IRCTC train tickets one can book at a time?

A: As per IRCTC, up to 12 bookings are allowed per month and up to 6 seats/berths can be booked per ticket. So, up to 36 tickets can be booked in a month from a single IRCTC User ID.

Q. How can I recover my IRCTC user ID and password?

A: You can find your login details in the IRCTC welcome message sent to your registered email. To locate this mail, use search terms like 'iRCTC' or 'ticketadmin@irctc.co.in' in your inbox. If you don't have this mail and wish to reset your password, go to the IRCTC website, then log in and then click on Forgot Password. Submit the required details and enter the OTP received via email/SMS to be led to a new page. Enter your new password here, and click on 'Update Password'to complete the process. Your username can be recovered similarly, by visiting irctc.co.in. Then Login and click on Forgot User ID. The existing user ID will be sent to your registered email.

Q. How can I book railway tickets by mobile?

A: If a railway booking is confirmed, it can be cancelled up to 48 hours before the scheduled departure of the train. As per the IRCTC website, tickets booked online cannot be cancelled after the chart has been prepared.

Q. Are children charged for IRCTC train ticket booking?

A: Children under 5 years of age are not charged for a train journey. Children between 5 to 12 years of age are charged fully if you have opted for a berth separately. However, if a berth/seat is not required for a child between 5 to 12 years of age, then only half of the adult traveller fare will be applicable.

Q. What is TDR in your IRCTC train ticket?

A: TDR refers to the ticket deposit receipt. Passengers who have a ticket but have not travelled, and want to apply for a refund, can file for a TDR. The IRCTC provides facilities to file TDR online. If the train chart is not prepared, users can cancel their ticket online. It is only after a chart is ready that a TDR needs to be filed for a refund. Some different scenarios where an IRCTC TDR can be filed are:

  • AC failure in train coaches.
  • No room for passengers after chart preparation.
  • Change in reservation status from confirmed to Waitlisted/Part Waitlisted/RAC after chart preparation.
  • Fare difference if the passenger travelled in a lower class.
  • Fare difference in case a proper coach is not attached.
  • Passenger could not cancel because the chart was prepared at the originating station or a previous remote location.
  • Partially Confirmed/Waitlisted tickets, but all passengers did not travel.
  • Partially Confirmed/Waitlisted tickets and waitlisted passengers did not travel.
  • Passengers partially travelled, before the journey was terminated short of destination.
  • Passenger did not travel as reservation was provided in a lower class.
  • Passenger did not travel due to the RAC ticket after chart preparation.
  • Train being diverted, so it cannot touch the boarding station.
  • Train being diverted, so it cannot touch the destination station.
  • Train was more than 3 hours late and the passengers were not travelling on it.
  • Train missed as the connecting train was late.
  • Train terminated before the destination.
  • Incorrectly charged by the TTE.
